Jesse Casman

Casman is a veteran marketing executive, with special expertise in open source and security. Most recently, Casman worked with multiple open source and development companies at Page One PR (now called LEWIS PR) in San Francisco. Casman has led marketing at two venture-funded security start-ups as well as a graphics software company spun out of SGI and later spun out technology that was acquired by Google to become Google Earth and is also used to Google Maps and Google Mobile. He has also served as acting president of an international Linux company based in Tokyo. Earlier in his career he was a technology analyst covering open source software, security, and the international technology market. He is the co-founder of Kachina Technology, a systems integration company in the United States that specialized in creating e-commerce web sites built on open source software. He holds a BA from Connecticut College and a graduate degree from Stanford University.
